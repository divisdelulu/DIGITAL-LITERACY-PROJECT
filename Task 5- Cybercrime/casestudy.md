# Case Study: Ransomware Attack on a Small Business
## Introduction
Ransomware is cyber-crime in which a hacker prevents access to a system or data and offers to remove it if a sum of money is paid. Currently it is among the most prevalent and dangerous cyber issues.

## Situation.

 A small accounting company was the victim of a ransomware infection.  Someone inside the company received an email believed to be from a reputable client. The email contained an attachment called “Invoice_Details.pdf”. The recipient opened the file which then covertly installed a trojan ransomware.

How the attack happened (step by step)

1. The employee opened the malicious email attachment.
2. Ransomware software was installed without the user‘s knowledge.
3. The malware spread across the company’s network.
4. All important files were encrypted and became inaccessible.
5. A ransom note appeared, demanding payment in cryptocurrency to unlock the files.
## Targeted Victims
Small and medium-sized enterprises are a common target of cyber-crimes because they typically lack security measures and are less-aware of security threats.

## Effects

The company no longer had access to the types of financial information they relied on daily.  The company lost revenue because of outages, while contemplating whether to pay the ransom.  In addition,  the company was damaged on their reputation basis,  as clients no longer trusted them in safeguarding sensitive data. Even when paying, there was no certainty that the data would be recovered.

## Conclusion
This can be seen as underlines the significance of cybersecurity awareness,  not opening spam e-mails and backing up date regularly.
